Courses Taught: MTC 245 (Chromatic Harmony I) and MTC 246 (Chromatic Harmony II)

Course Design and Delivery:

  • Independently designed and delivered lectures on advanced topics in music theory, including: Ternary Piano Pieces: Taught students how to analyze and compose ternary-form piano pieces, emphasizing structural analysis, harmonic progression, and thematic development. Integrated music appreciation to provide historical and stylistic context. Underset Theory: Introduced students to pitch-class sets and their application in analyzing the music of Alexander Scriabin and Karol Szymanowski. Guided students through complex harmonic structures and modernist compositional techniques.
  • Developed lesson plansand teaching materials (e.g., handouts, assignments, etc.) tailored to course objectives and student needs.
  • Assessed student work, provided detailed feedback, and facilitated discussions to deepen understanding of theoretical concepts.

Classroom Management:

  • Created an inclusive and engaging learning environment by encouraging active participation and critical thinking.
  • Adapted teaching methods to accommodate diverse learning styles and levels of prior knowledge.
  • Managed classroom dynamics, resolved student inquiries, and ensured alignment with course goals.

Baroque to 20th-Century Composers: Taught students how to analyze and interpret works by composers such as Bach, Beethoven, Debussy, and Stravinsky, integrating historical context, theoretical frameworks, and performance practice.
